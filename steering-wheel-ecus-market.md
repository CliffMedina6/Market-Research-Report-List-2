<p><p>Steering Wheel ECUs (Electronic Control Units) are integral components in modern vehicles that control the various functions related to steering. There are three types of Steering Wheel ECUs in the market: REPS (Rack Electronic Power Steering), CEPS (Column Electronic Power Steering), and PEPS (Power Electronic Power Steering). REPS systems utilize a steering rack to provide power assistance, while CEPS systems integrate the power assist mechanism into the steering column. PEPS systems, on the other hand, leverage power electronics to offer power assistance directly to the steering wheel. These different types cater to diverse vehicle requirements and deliver varying steering performance.</p></p>
