<p><p>Pretreatment auxiliaries refer to various chemicals used in the textile industry to prepare fabrics before dyeing or printing. Activator agents are substances that activate dye fixation, ensuring better color retention. Desizing agents are used to remove sizing agents (starches or other additives) from fabrics to improve dyeing results. Dispersant agents help disperse and stabilize dyes or pigment particles in solution. Cleaner agents aid in the removal of impurities, such as oil or dirt, from fabric surfaces. Other types of pretreatment auxiliaries include wetting agents, sequestering agents, and leveling agents, which assist in different stages of fabric preparation.</p></p>
