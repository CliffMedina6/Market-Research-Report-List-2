<p><p>Metal-matrix composites (MMCs) are advanced materials that combine a metal matrix with reinforcement materials to enhance their properties. Various types of MMCs exist in the market: </p><p>1. Powder Metallurgy MMCs are made by blending metal powders with reinforcement particles and compacting them into desired shapes using heat and pressure.</p><p>2. Liquid-permeable MMCs are produced using a porous ceramic network infiltrated with molten metal, creating a strong and lightweight composite.</p><p>3. Foundry MMCs are fabricated by adding reinforcement materials into the molten metal during the casting process, leading to improved mechanical properties.</p><p>4. Deposition Technology MMCs involve depositing reinforcement material onto the metal matrix through techniques such as physical vapor deposition or electrodeposition, resulting in excellent control over properties like strength and conductivity.</p></p>
