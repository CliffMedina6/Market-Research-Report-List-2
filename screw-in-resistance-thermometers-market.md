<p><p>Screw-in resistance thermometers are devices used to measure temperature in various industries. They are commonly categorized into three types based on the wiring configuration: 2 wire, 3 wire, and 4 wire. </p><p>In the 2 wire system, the thermometer is connected using two wires, which can cause some inaccuracies due to resistance in the wires. </p><p>The 3 wire system helps compensate for this resistance by using an additional wire, improving accuracy. </p><p>The 4 wire system further enhances accuracy by having two wires for carrying current and the other two for measuring resistance. This configuration eliminates any errors caused by wire resistance, resulting in highly accurate temperature measurements.</p></p>
