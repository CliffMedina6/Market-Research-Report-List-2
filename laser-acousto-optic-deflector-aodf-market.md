<p><p>A Laser Acousto-Optic Deflector (AODF) is a device used to control the direction of laser beams. The market for AODFs can be divided into two types: Free-Space Acousto-Optic Deflectors and Fiber-Coupled Acousto-Optic Deflectors. </p><p>Free-Space AODFs are designed for use in open spaces and can deflect laser beams without the need for fiber optics. They offer flexibility in beam control and are commonly used in scientific research and industrial applications.</p><p>On the other hand, Fiber-Coupled AODFs are specifically designed for integration with fiber optic systems. They provide a means to change the direction of laser beams within fiber optic cables, making them ideal for telecommunications and fiber laser applications.</p><p>Both types of AODFs cater to different market needs and have their own unique advantages and applications in the laser industry.</p></p>
