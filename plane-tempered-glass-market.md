<p><p>Plane tempered glass, also known as flat tempered glass, is a type of safety glass that has been treated by thermal or chemical processes to increase its strength. It undergoes a heating and rapid cooling process, which adds compressive stress to the surface of the glass, making it four to five times stronger than regular annealed glass. When broken, plane tempered glass shatters into small, relatively harmless pieces, reducing the risk of injury.</p><p>The plane tempered glass market has witnessed significant growth in recent years, driven by the increasing demand for safety glass in various industries such as construction, automotive, and electronics. The rising awareness regarding the benefits of tempered glass, such as enhanced strength, safety, and durability, has also contributed to market growth. Additionally, the growing trend of utilizing glass in interior design applications and the rising construction activities across the globe have further boosted market growth.</p><p>Furthermore, technological advancements in the manufacturing processes of plane tempered glass have led to improved product quality and reduced production costs, which has positively impacted market growth.
