<p><p>The Fiber Optic Preform Market comprises various types, including Vapor Axial Deposition (VAD), Outside Vapor Deposition (OVD), Plasma Chemical Vapor Deposition (PCVD), and Modified Chemical Vapor Deposition (MCVD). VAD involves depositing multiple layers of glass over a rotating starting rod. OVD involves heating a porous preform through a burner while depositing soot on it. PCVD employs plasma energy to deposit glass on a cylindrical preform. Lastly, MCVD uses the vapor phase reaction between gases to deposit glass on the inner surface of a preform. These processes are used to manufacture fiber optic preforms.</p></p>
