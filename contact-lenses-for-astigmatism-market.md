<p><p>Contact lenses for astigmatism are specially designed lenses that correct the blurry vision caused by a common eye condition called astigmatism. Astigmatism occurs when the cornea or lens of the eye has an irregular shape, causing the light entering the eye to be refracted in multiple directions, resulting in distorted vision.</p><p>These contact lenses have a toric design, which means they have different powers in different meridians of the lens to compensate for the irregularity. This design ensures that the lens sits properly on the eye and aligns with the astigmatic axis, providing clear and sharp vision.</p><p>The future outlook of the contact lenses for astigmatism market is positive. The increasing prevalence of astigmatism and advancements in technology are driving the demand for these specialized lenses. Additionally, the growing awareness about the benefits of contact lenses over traditional eyeglasses for astigmatism management is further fueling market growth.</p><p>Moreover, factors such as the rising geriatric population, increasing disposable income, and the expanding global population are expected to contribute to the growth of the market. <p><p>Contact lenses for astigmatism come in different types such as conventional hydrogel material and highly breathable silicone hydrogel. Conventional hydrogel lenses are made of a soft material that allows oxygen to pass through to the eyes. They are suitable for individuals with low to moderate astigmatism. On the other hand, highly breathable silicone hydrogel lenses are made of a more advanced material that provides excellent oxygen permeability. These lenses are recommended for individuals with moderate to high astigmatism levels and offer enhanced comfort and clear vision.</p></p>
