<p><ul><li>Isotropic</li><li>Anisotropic</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Electrically Conductive Epoxy Market consists of two types: isotropic and anisotropic. Isotropic electrically conductive epoxy has the same electrical conductivity in all directions, making it suitable for applications where electrical charge needs to flow uniformly. Anisotropic electrically conductive epoxy, on the other hand, has different electrical conductivity in different directions, making it ideal for applications that require controlled electrical conductivity along specific pathways. Both types are used in various industries such as electronics, automotive, and aerospace, where the ability to conduct electricity is crucial for the functionality of the products.</p></p>
