<p><p>Galvanized steel is a type of steel that is coated with a layer of zinc to protect it from corrosion. There are two main types of galvanized steel: hot-dip galvanized steel and electrical galvanized steel. Hot-dip galvanized steel involves immersing the steel in a bath of molten zinc, while electrical galvanized steel involves applying a zinc coating to the steel through an electroplating process. Both types provide excellent protection against rust and are commonly used in applications where durability and corrosion resistance are important.</p></p>
