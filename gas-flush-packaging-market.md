<p><p>Gas flush packaging, also known as modified atmosphere packaging (MAP), is a popular packaging technique used to extend the shelf life of perishable food products. This method involves removing the oxygen content from the packaging and replacing it with a gas mixture, usually nitrogen, carbon dioxide, or a combination of both. By reducing the oxygen levels, gas flush packaging inhibits the growth of bacteria, yeast, and molds, thereby enhancing the product's freshness and quality.</p><p>The global gas flush packaging market is projected to witness significant growth in the coming years. Factors such as increasing consumer preference for hygienic and fresh food products, the rise in the demand for convenience foods, and the growing awareness about food wastage reduction are driving market growth.
