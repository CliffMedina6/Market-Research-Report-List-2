<p><p>Leach fields, also known as drain fields or absorption fields, are components of septic systems used for the treatment and disposal of wastewater. They consist of a series of perforated pipes buried in trenches filled with gravel or other porous material. The wastewater flows from the septic tank into the leach field, where it percolates through the soil, undergoing biological and physical processes that remove harmful bacteria, viruses, and other contaminants.</p><p>The global leach fields market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years. The increasing population, urbanization, and industrialization are leading to a rising demand for effective wastewater management solutions, boosting the adoption of septic systems and, consequently, the leach fields market.
