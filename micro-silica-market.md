<p><p>Micro silica, also known as silica fume or condensed silica fume, is a byproduct of the production of silicon metal or ferrosilicon alloys. It is composed of very fine particles of amorphous silica, which are approximately 100 times smaller than a cement particle. Micro silica offers numerous benefits in various applications due to its unique properties, such as high pozzolanic activity, increased compressive strength, reduced permeability, and improved durability.</p><p>The global micro silica market is expected to witness significant growth during the forecast period. One of the primary factors driving the market growth is the rising demand for high-performance concrete in the construction industry. Micro silica enhances the strength and durability of the concrete, making it suitable for infrastructure projects such as bridges, tunnels, and high-rise buildings. The growing construction activities, particularly in developing countries, and the increasing focus on sustainable construction practices are fueling the demand for micro silica.</p><p>Moreover, the increasing use of micro silica in refractories, ceramics, electronic components, rubber, and plastics is also contributing to market growth. <p><p>Micro silica, also known as silica fume, is a finely powdered material used as a performance-enhancing additive in concrete and cementitious products. It is available in three different market types: densified micro silica, semi-densified micro silica, and undensified micro silica. </p><p>Densified micro silica is produced by treating the silica fume with chemicals to increase its bulk density, making it easier to handle and transport. </p><p>Semi-densified micro silica has a lower bulk density compared to densified micro silica and is partially treated with chemicals. </p><p>Undensified micro silica has the lowest bulk density and is in its raw form without any chemical treatment.</p></p>
