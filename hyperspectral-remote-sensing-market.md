<p><p>Hyperspectral remote sensing technology is divided into three market types based on different wavelength ranges. VNIR (Visible and Near-Infrared) captures information in the visible and nearby infrared spectrum, enabling analysis of vegetation health, mineralogy, and water quality. SWIR (Short-Wave Infrared) detects reflected light in shorter wavelengths, allowing identification of specific materials and enhanced geological mapping. Thermal LWIR (Long-Wave Infrared) focuses on thermal radiation emitted by objects, facilitating monitoring of environmental temperature, gas emissions, and industrial thermography. These market segments cater to diverse applications in sectors such as agriculture, environmental monitoring, and defense.</p></p>
