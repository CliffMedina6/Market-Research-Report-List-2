<p><p>Alginic acid market primarily consists of three types: sodium alginate, potassium alginate, and calcium alginate. Sodium alginate is derived from brown seaweed and is widely used as a thickening agent and stabilizer in various industries such as food, pharmaceuticals, and textiles. Potassium alginate, also extracted from seaweed, possesses similar properties but is often preferred in industries where potassium compatibility is desired. Calcium alginate, on the other hand, is derived from brown algae and is commonly used in the medical field, particularly in wound dressings and as a tissue engineering scaffold.</p></p>
