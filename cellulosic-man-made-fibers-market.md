<p><p>Cellulosic man-made fibers are synthetic fibers derived from cellulose found in plants. Acetate fibers are made by treating cellulose with acetic acid, resulting in a fabric with a silk-like texture. Triacetate fibers are produced from cellulose acetate, but with an additional chemical treatment to increase durability and wrinkle resistance. Viscose fibers, also known as rayon, are made by dissolving cellulose in solvents, then regenerating it into fibers. Viscose fibers are versatile and commonly used in clothing, upholstery, and household textiles for their softness and breathability.</p></p>
