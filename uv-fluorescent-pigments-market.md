<p><ul><li>Thermoset Type</li><li>Thermoplastic Type</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The UV fluorescent pigments market is categorized into three types: thermoset type, thermoplastic type, and others. </p><p>Thermoset type UV fluorescent pigments are suitable for applications that require durability and resistance to high temperatures. They can be cured through a chemical reaction, resulting in a rigid and resilient finish. </p><p>Thermoplastic type UV fluorescent pigments, on the other hand, can be melted and reshaped multiple times without undergoing any significant chemical change. They offer flexibility and are commonly used in applications that involve molding or extrusion processes. </p><p>Lastly, the "others" category includes UV fluorescent pigments that do not fall under the thermoset or thermoplastic types, such as hybrid formulations or unique compositions.</p></p>
