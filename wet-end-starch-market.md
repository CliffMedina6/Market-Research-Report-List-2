<p><p>Wet-end starch is used in the papermaking process to improve paper strength and performance. Cationic starch is a type of wet-end starch that carries a positive charge, making it effective in enhancing paper retention and formation. It helps in reducing fiber loss and improving drainage. Amphoteric starch, on the other hand, is a type of wet-end starch that carries both positive and negative charges. It provides versatility and is used when a more balanced approach is needed in papermaking, offering improved strength and fixing properties.</p></p>
