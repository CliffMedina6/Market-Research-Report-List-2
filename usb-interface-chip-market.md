<p><p>A USB Interface Chip is an integrated circuit that provides the necessary functionality for connecting devices to a computer system using the Universal Serial Bus (USB) standard. The demand for higher data transfer rates, improved power management, and compatibility with multiple USB standards has further fueled the market growth. One such trend is the development of USB 3.0 and USB 3.1 standards, which offer faster data transfer rates and improved power delivery capabilities.
