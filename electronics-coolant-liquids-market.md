<p><p>The electronics coolant liquids market consists of two types: single phase and two-phase. Single-phase coolants are liquids that remain in one state of matter, either liquid or gas, to remove heat efficiently from electronic components. They are commonly used in low-temperature applications. In contrast, two-phase coolants transition between liquid and gas phases, effectively transporting heat from high-temperature components to cool them down. Two-phase coolants are often preferred in high-performance electronics where heat dissipation is crucial for efficient operation.</p></p>
