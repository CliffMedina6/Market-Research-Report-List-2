<p><p>Real-time power factor correction systems are devices used to improve power factor in electrical systems by reducing reactive power. There are two main types of markets for such systems: 1-phase and 3-phase. </p><p>In a 1-phase system, the power factor correction system is designed to compensate for the reactive power in a single-phase power supply. This is typically used in residential and small commercial applications.</p><p>In a 3-phase system, the power factor correction system is designed to compensate for the reactive power in a three-phase power supply. This is commonly used in larger industrial and commercial applications where higher loads are present.</p><p>Both types of systems work by automatically detecting the power factor and adjusting the electrical parameters to optimize power factor and reduce energy costs.</p></p>
