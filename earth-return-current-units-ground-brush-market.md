<p><p>Earth return current units, also known as ground brush, are electrical devices used in electrical power systems to safely disperse unwanted currents into the ground. They come in different market types: axial and radial. Axial market devices consist of a conducting cylinder surrounding a grounded conductor, while radial market devices feature concentric rings surrounding a central electrode. Axial market units are effective for large surface areas, such as power plant substations, while radial market units are suitable for smaller grounding applications, like residential or commercial buildings. Both types aid in maintaining electrical safety and preventing harm to people and equipment.</p></p>
