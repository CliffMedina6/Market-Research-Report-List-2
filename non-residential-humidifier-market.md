<p><p>The non-residential humidifier market can be divided into two types: adiabatic and isothermal humidifiers. Adiabatic humidifiers operate by evaporating water into the air, increasing its humidity. They are effective in arid climates but are less energy-efficient. Isothermal humidifiers, on the other hand, add moisture to the air by heating water to create steam. They are more energy-efficient but require a constant supply of electricity. Both types serve the purpose of increasing humidity in non-residential spaces but differ in their methods of achieving this.</p></p>
