<p><p>The food binders market is categorized into four types based on their composition and functionality. Sugar binders are derived from sugar and are commonly used in confectionery products. Starch binders are derived from various sources like corn, potato, and wheat and are used in sauces and dressings for thickening purposes. Protein binders, typically derived from animal or plant sources, are used in meat products for binding and improving texture. Lastly, gel binders are made from gelling agents like agar or carrageenan and are used for creating jelly-like textures in desserts and dairy products.</p></p>
