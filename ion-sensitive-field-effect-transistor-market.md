It operates similarly to a conventional metal-oxide-semiconductor field-effect transistor (MOSFET), with the key difference being the gate material. In ISFETs, the gate is typically covered with a pH-sensitive material, such as silicon dioxide doped with metal ions. <p><ul><li>JFET Type</li><li>MOS Type</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Ion Sensitive Field Effect Transistor (ISFET) market encompasses two main types: JFET Type and MOS Type. JFET Type ISFETs use a junction field-effect transistor as the sensing element. They operate based on the modulation of an induced channel caused by the presence of ions in the solution being sensed. On the other hand, MOS Type ISFETs employ a metal-oxide-semiconductor as their sensing element. These ISFETs operate by measuring the surface potential changes caused by ion adsorption at the oxide-electrolyte interface. Both types are widely used in various applications that require ion sensing and monitoring.</p></p>
