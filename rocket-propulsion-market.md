<p><p>Rocket propulsion is categorized into three types: solid propulsion, liquid propulsion, and hybrid propulsion. Solid propulsion systems use solid fuels, which are ignited and burn within a fixed structure. Liquid propulsion systems involve liquid fuels combined with oxidizers, which are mixed and ignited to produce thrust. Hybrid propulsion systems combine solid and liquid fuels, where one propellant is solid and the other is liquid. Each propulsion system has its advantages and limitations, depending on factors such as performance, efficiency, cost, and safety considerations.</p></p>
