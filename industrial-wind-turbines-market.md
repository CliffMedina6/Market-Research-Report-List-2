<p><p>Industrial wind turbines are categorized into two main types: horizontal axis wind turbines (HAWT) and vertical axis wind turbines (VAWT). HAWTs have blades that rotate on a horizontal axis, capturing wind energy from any direction. These turbines are commonly used in large-scale wind farms due to their high energy output. On the other hand, VAWTs have blades that rotate on a vertical axis, enabling them to harness wind energy even in turbulent or inconsistent wind conditions. VAWTs are often preferred for smaller-scale applications or urban areas due to their design flexibility and compact size.</p></p>
