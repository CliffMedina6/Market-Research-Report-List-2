<p><p>The mashed potatoes market consists of two main types: powder mashed potatoes and solid mashed potatoes. Powder mashed potatoes are dehydrated and come in powdered form, requiring rehydration with water or milk before consumption. They offer convenience and a longer shelf life. On the other hand, solid mashed potatoes are prepared by mashing cooked potatoes, usually with added butter and milk, and are sold refrigerated or frozen. Solid mashed potatoes offer a more traditional and homemade taste and texture. Both types cater to different consumer preferences and are widely available in the market.</p></p>
