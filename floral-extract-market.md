<p><p>The floral extract market is divided into two types: powder and liquid. Powdered floral extracts are created by drying and grinding flowers into a fine powder form, which is then used in various products like cosmetics, perfumes, or supplements. Liquid floral extracts, on the other hand, are obtained by macerating flowers in solvents like water or alcohol to extract the essential oils and active compounds. These liquid extracts are commonly used in aromatherapy, skincare products, and herbal medicines. Both powder and liquid floral extracts have diverse applications and are sought after for their unique sensory and therapeutic properties.</p></p>
