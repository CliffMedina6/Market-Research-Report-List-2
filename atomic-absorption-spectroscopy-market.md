<p><p>Atomic absorption spectroscopy (AAS) is commonly used in various industries, and it can be divided into different market types. The first is the flame AAS market, where the sample is atomized by a flame. This technique is suitable for analyzing elements with low ionization energies. The second market type is the graphite furnace AAS, which involves vaporizing the sample using a high-temperature graphite furnace. This technique is ideal for analyzing trace elements in complex samples. Lastly, the "other" market type refers to alternative AAS methods, such as hydride generation AAS or cold vapor AAS, which are employed for specific applications or unique experimental conditions.</p></p>
