<p><p>Protective paints refer to coatings that are applied on various surfaces to protect them from environmental factors such as corrosion, UV radiation, and moisture. There are three main types of protective paints in the market: solvent-borne coatings, water-borne coatings, and powder coatings. Solvent-borne coatings use organic solvents as a medium for the paint, providing good adhesion and resistance to chemicals. Water-borne coatings, on the other hand, use water as the medium, making them environmentally friendly and easy to apply. Lastly, powder coatings are applied as dry powder and then cured with heat to create a durable and decorative finish.</p></p>
