<p><p>Die Attach Adhesive Film is a material used in the semiconductor industry to attach dies (small electronic components) to substrates. The market for this film can be divided into two types: non-conductive and conductive. Non-conductive adhesive films provide electrical insulation between the die and the substrate, preventing unwanted current flow. On the other hand, conductive adhesive films allow electrical conductivity, enabling the flow of current between the die and the substrate. Both types serve specific purposes, depending on the application requirements in the semiconductor industry.</p></p>
