<p><p>Model train market types refer to the different electrical systems used in model train layouts. DC Current Two Rails systems use a direct current and have two parallel metal rails, where the locomotive picks up power through the wheels and transfers it to the motor. AC Current Three Rails systems use an alternating current and have three metal rails, where one rail carries the AC power and the other two act as conductors. These types differ in terms of power supply and rail configuration, resulting in distinctions in model trains and accessories.</p></p>
